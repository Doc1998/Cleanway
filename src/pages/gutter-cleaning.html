Our Process for Cleaning Gutters

We carefully inspect your gutters and downspouts, looking for any visible debris or obstructions. Using gloves, we remove loose debris by hand or with a small garden trowel. If needed, we employ a gutter scoop to gently loosen and remove stubborn debris without damaging the gutters.

Our Gutter Cleaning Tools

Gutter Scoop: A gutter scoop is a specialized tool designed to help remove debris from gutters. It typically has a curved shape that allows it to fit inside the gutter and scoop out leaves, twigs, and other debris effectively.

Gutter Cleaning Wand: A gutter cleaning wand is an extension tool that connects to a garden hose. It features a curved or angled nozzle at the end, allowing you to spray water directly into the gutters to dislodge and flush out debris. Some wands may also have adjustable water pressure settings.

Pressure Washer: In certain situations and for suitable gutter types, professionals may use a pressure washer to clean gutters. With a low-pressure setting, a pressure washer can effectively remove stubborn debris and clogs. However, it's essential to exercise caution and follow safety guidelines when using a pressure washer.

Gutter Repairs and Roof Cleaning

A dirty roof can lead to leaks, damage to the roof and gutter clogging

Clogging: As debris from a dirty roof flows into the gutters, it can accumulate and form clogs. These clogs obstruct the flow of water through the gutters, causing water to overflow or pool. Clogged gutters can result in water damage to the roof, fascia, and even the foundation of your home.

Moisture and Rot: A dirty roof can retain moisture, especially if moss or algae growth is present. Excessive moisture can seep into the fascia, soffit, and roof structure. This moisture exposure can lead to rot, deterioration, and damage to these components, ultimately affecting the gutters that are attached to them.

Ice Dams: In colder climates, a dirty roof can contribute to the formation of ice dams. When debris blocks proper water drainage, melted snow or ice can accumulate along the roof's edge and freeze. These ice dams can prevent proper water flow into the gutters, leading to potential roof leaks and damage.

To ensure the optimal functioning of your gutters, it's essential to keep your roof clean and free from debris. Regular roof maintenance and gutter cleaning go hand in hand, as they both contribute to a healthy and efficient water drainage system.
