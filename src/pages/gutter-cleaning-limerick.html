Warning Signs

8 Signs Your Gutters Need Professional Cleaning

Ignoring blocked gutters leads to expensive damage. If you notice any of these warning signs, it's time to call Cleanway for a professional gutter clean in Limerick.

01

Water Overflowing From Gutters

If rainwater spills over the edge of your gutters during rainfall rather than flowing to the downpipe, your gutters are blocked. This overflow causes water to run down external walls, leading to damp penetration and paint damage.

02

Sagging or Pulling Away

Gutters filled with wet debris become extremely heavy. Over time, this weight causes brackets to loosen and gutters to pull away from the fascia. Left untreated, sections can collapse entirely — requiring expensive replacement.

03

Plants or Weeds Growing

If you can see grass, weeds, or small plants sprouting from your gutters, it means there's enough compacted soil and organic matter to support growth. This is a clear sign of a significant blockage that needs professional vacuum cleaning.

04

Staining on External Walls

Green or black streaks running down your walls beneath the gutterline indicate water has been overflowing for some time. This constant moisture leads to algae growth, rendering damage, and eventually damp issues inside the property.

05

Damp Patches Inside Your Home

Damp spots appearing on bedroom ceilings or upper-floor walls near the roofline often trace back to overflowing gutters. Water finds its way behind fascia boards and into the roofspace, causing mould and structural damage.

06

Birds Nesting in Your Gutters

Gutters filled with leaves and moss make attractive nesting spots for birds. Nesting material causes severe blockages, and bird droppings add to the problem. Our vacuum system removes all nesting material safely.

07

Pooling Water Around Foundations

When gutters overflow, water pools around the base of your home instead of being directed away through the drainage system. This can undermine foundations and cause cracking, subsidence, and flood risk to ground-floor rooms.

08

You Haven't Cleaned Them in Over a Year

Even if you don't see visible problems, Limerick's wet climate and mature tree canopy mean gutters accumulate debris quickly. Annual cleaning prevents small build-ups from becoming major blockages and costly repairs.

Everything You Need to Know About Gutter Cleaning in Limerick

Why Gutter Cleaning Matters in Limerick's Climate

Limerick receives well above the national average rainfall, with over 1,000mm annually. Combined with frequent Atlantic storms and the mature trees found across the city's residential areas, gutters in Limerick face a relentless onslaught of leaves, moss, and wind-blown debris throughout the year.

When gutters become blocked, rainwater has nowhere to go. It overflows down the face of your walls, seeps behind fascia boards, and pools around your foundations. In Limerick's damp climate, this excess moisture quickly leads to penetrating damp, black mould growth in bedrooms and living areas, and in severe cases, structural damage to timber roof components.

The cost of repairing damp damage — replastering walls, replacing rotted fascia, treating mould — far exceeds the cost of an annual gutter clean. Most Limerick homeowners find that regular gutter maintenance is one of the most cost-effective ways to protect their property.

What Happens During a Cleanway Gutter Clean

When our team arrives at your property, we may begin with a visual inspection of your guttering system from ground level using our camera-equipped poles. This allows us to assess the level of blockage and identify any damage or issues before we start cleaning.

We then use our high reach vacuum system to methodically work around the entire property, clearing every section of guttering. The powerful suction removes all debris — from loose leaves and moss to compacted silt and roofing grit that's built up over months or years.

If we encounter a downpipe blockage, we clear it using our specialist equipment. Finally, we carry out a second camera pass to confirm every section of guttering is completely clear.

Before we leave, we can provide you with photos as proof of the work completed. If we've identified any damage — cracked joints, loose brackets, corroded sections — we'll let you knowS

How Often Should You Clean Your Gutters in Limerick?

As a minimum, we recommend an annual gutter clean — typically in late autumn (November) once most leaves have fallen. However, many Limerick homes benefit from twice-yearly cleaning: once in autumn and once in spring. This is especially true for properties surrounded by deciduous trees, homes near farmland, older properties with cast-iron guttering, or houses in exposed locations that collect wind-blown debris.

If you've recently had roofing work done, we also recommend a gutter clean to remove tile grit, mortar, and roofing felt that inevitably ends up in the gutters during construction.

Fascia and Soffit Cleaning: The Full Roofline Treatment

While you're having your gutters cleaned, it makes sense to clean your fascia and soffits at the same time. Fascia boards — the flat boards running along the lower edge of your roof — and soffits — the underside panels beneath the roofline — are constantly exposed to the elements. In Limerick's damp climate, they quickly develop black streaks, green algae, and a general dull, weathered appearance.

Our fascia and soffit cleaning service uses a soft-wash system with specialist cleaning solutions that are safe for uPVC, painted wood, and aluminium finishes. We don't use pressure washers on fascia and soffits — high pressure can force water behind boards and damage seals. Instead, our low-pressure, high-reach approach delivers a deep clean that restores your roofline to its original colour without any risk of damage.

Most customers who book fascia and soffit cleaning in Limerick are genuinely surprised at the difference. Years of weathering can make white uPVC look grey or yellow — after our clean, it looks factory-fresh again, dramatically improving the kerb appeal of your home.
