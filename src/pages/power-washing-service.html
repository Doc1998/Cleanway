    Frequently Asked Questions

    • Pressure washing is a cleaning method that uses high-pressure water spray to remove dirt, grime, mold, mildew, and other contaminants from surfaces. This technique is commonly used on driveways, patios, decks, and building exteriors.

    • The frequency of pressure washing depends on various factors, including the local climate, environmental conditions, and the specific surfaces being cleaned. Generally, it is recommended to pressure wash your property at least once a year. However, high-traffic areas or properties in regions with heavy rainfall or high pollen counts may require more frequent cleaning.

    • When done correctly, pressure washing should not damage surfaces. However, using too high a pressure setting or an inappropriate nozzle can cause harm, especially to delicate surfaces like wood or certain types of siding. Professional pressure washing services use the correct settings and techniques to ensure effective cleaning without damaging your property.
